PERSONAL REFLECTION

Suggested Book Title;

Illegal by Birth, Unstopabble by Will

This title fits in for the book as Trevor Noah tries to express his birth to be somewhat illegal. This
is seen from when he was born as the mother had to lie in the hospital and also she had to
give birth in a different place so that the baby, Trevor Noah, can be accepted in the society.
Additionally, in the book, Trevor Noah describes how he receives a lot of bullying especially
when they moved to Eden park where kids could not play with him just because he was
coloured. All this shows that his existence was outlawed and he was not supposed to exist
in a world that worked with either the black people, the white people and not anybody in
between.

Amidst all this, Trevor Noah is unstoppable by will since his determination and also character
made him unstoppable in achieving what he wanted. Even in a society that was trying to
dim him, he stood in the midst of it all, learnt different languages and also tried too hard to
work as he was also navigating his highschool journey. Him lacking his place in the society
did not stop him from achieving the best and being the best in whatever she does.

Reflective Blog

"Abel wanted a traditional marriage with a traditional wife… but he never fell in love
with subservient women… 'He only wants a woman who is free because his
dream is to put her in a cage.'”

This quote from Born a crime resonates with our today's society as this still exists in the
society. When Patricia says that, “He only wants a woman who is free because his
dream is to put her in a cage” go hand in hand with how men admire strong and
independent women and not until they are in a relationship with them that they try to try
controlling them so as to put their dreams in a cage. This shows that the very traits they
loved become a threat to their control.

Abel adored how Patricia was independent and strong but he could not handle it when his
independence and strength questioned his authority. This is a gender dynamic that is
seen even in society today. Womens strength and independence is always celebrated
until it seems not to go with the societal expectations like being submissive or
prioritizing career to family. In our today's society, especially through social media, most
men will praise a woman because she is independent and going above and beyond but
still behind the lights shame her and try to insinuate that they are too opinionated or
they are too ambitious to be loved. This shows that some people are still holding on to
outdated expectations while others are ready for equality.

Personally, I have seen women who have shrinked themselves so that they can fit in
relationships and also marriages. They end up hiding their success and their strength
so as to fit in. This just shows the misinterpretation of true love as love should not
require one to hide under a cage but should allow people to celebrate growth and
freedom. As a society, there is a need to redefine the meaning of strength and also
partnership. Relationships and marriage should be all about mutual respect, and we
should all not fall in love with people to contain them but as they are.

MEET THE AUTHOR

"We tell people to follow their dreams, but you can only dream of what you
can imagine, and, depending on where you come from, your imagination can
be quite limited."
-Trevor Noah

Trevor Noah was born on 20 February 1984, in Johannesburg, Transvaal (now
Gauteng), South Africa.His father, Robert, is Swiss-German, and his mother, Patricia
Nombuyiselo Noah, is Xhosa. Noah is a South African comedian, television host,
actor, and political commentator. He is the host of The Daily Show, an American
satirical news program on Comedy Central.

Noah has won various awards, including an MTV Africa Music Award and a Primetime
Emmy Award from eleven nominations. He was named one of "The 35 Most
Powerful People in New York Media" by The Hollywood Reporter in 2017 and 2018.
In 2018, Time magazine named him one of the hundred most influential people in
the world.

He is the author of the #1 New York Times bestseller “Born a Crime: Stories from a
South African Childhood” and its young readers adaptation, released in 2019. To
date, “Born a Crime” has sold over 3 million copies across all formats. Following
the extraordinary success of “Born a Crime,” Trevor is writing a new book titled
“Into the Uncut Grass” for One World (an imprint of the Random House Group, a
division of Penguin Random House LLC). The book is scheduled for release in
2024 and is a gorgeously illustrated and moving modern fable for readers of all
ages about forgiveness, acceptance, and the secret to solidarity.
